Bharatanatyam

This is the classical dance of Tamil Nadu which is said to have originated from the temples by the temple-dancers called "Devadasis".They danced to bring out the praise of the Lord.Bharatanatyam is known for its sharp,sculptrous poses and the "Araimandi"(half sitting posture).This is performed both by men and women.Rukmini Devi Arundale in 1936 started the "Kalakshetra" near Thiruvanmiyur to spread the art form worldwide to prevent its extinction due to Britishers.

Mohiniyattam

This is one of the classical dances from Kerala.It is mainly known for its "Lasyam" ie more of feminity.This dance form is exclusively performed by women.Mohiniyattam is known for its graceful swaying movements just like paddy or coconut trees swaying with the wind.

Kuchipudi

This is the classical dance form of Andhra Pradesh,named after the village from where it is said to have originated.This village is situated in Krishna distt of AP.This was generally a temple-based drama-cum-dance performed only by men.Later,Kuchipudi saw a revolution by Vempati Chinnasatyam,he introduced women to Kuchipudi.Balancing pots on the head,holding diyas,dancing on brass plate iare the very unique features of Kuchipudi.

Odissi

This is the classical dance of Odisha said to have originated from the Jagannath Puri Temple.Two main styles "Mahaaris"(Ladies who never danced/came outside the temples and were said to be married to the Lord) and "Gotipua"(In Odia means "A boy",here boys lesser than 13 yrs of age dressed up as girls danced outside the temple with lot of acrobatics) are the basis of mordern Odissi dance.The S-shaped "Tribhangi" posture is the unique style of Odissi.
